Barcelona are still looking to strengthen their squad before the transfer window closes on Thursday and have enough financial wiggle room to complete further deals. Bar  a's fiscal woes over the last couple of years have been well documented and they aren't out of the woods just yet. They have been unable to register new signing Jules Kounde with La Liga thus far, though expect him to be available for Sunday's match at home to Real Valladolid. The like of Bernardo Silva and Marcos Alonso remain on Barcelona's radar, and Marca report that the club have roughly    57m left to spend on transfers in the coming days. Bar  a chiefs decided on a    200m transfer budget at the start of the summer, of which they have already spent    148m on signings such as Robert Lewandowski, Raphinha and the aforementioned Kounde, while they have been boosted by the    5m sale of Ferran Jutgla.
