Manchester United have confirmed the signing of Netherlands international Donny van de Beek for a fee of    39m, with a further    5m to come in add ons. While the midfielder has previously been linked with the Old Trafford side, United intensified their interest in recent weeks and have now signed off on the deal for the Ajax star. He has signed a five-year deal with an option for a further year. The added depth the signing will also bring offers the Norwegian another plus in what is expected to be a hectic and congested season for the Red Devils. Bruno Fernandes and Paul Pogba are United's two standout stars in midfield, so Van de Beek's arrival may mean more opportunities to rest the pair, while he will also compete for playing time with Nemanja Matic, Fred and Scott McTominay. It may also nudge Jesse Lingard and Andreas Pereira closer to the Old Trafford exit door. Speaking to the club's official website, Van de Beek said: "I cannot begin to explain how incredible an opportunity it is to join a club with such an amazing history. I would like to thank everyone at Ajax.
