Sergio Ramos has claimed that Kylian Mbappe is "very happy" at Paris Saint-Germain, despite widespread reports citing the Frenchman's desire to leave the club. 90min revealed on Wednesday that Mbappe is looking to engineer a move away from the Parc des Princes, citing broken promises made to him, just months after signing a new three-year contract. The 23-year-old would ideally like to move in January, with long-term admirers Real Madrid his most likely destination, but accepts that a transfer during the season will be difficult to execute. In any case, PSG have no interest in selling or loaning Mbappe. Manager Christophe Galtier has openly admitted that Mbappe isn't pleased with his role in the team, with Neymar and Lionel Messi currently operating in the deeper-lying positions that the World Cup winner would like to play in, and is looking at "different options" to try and please him. Mbappe's mood won't have been improved by his side's 1-1 draw with Benfica in the Champions League on Tuesday night, but Ramos stressed after the game his belief that the rumours about Mbappe wanting to leave are untrue.
